Korean Language Overview

Korean (한국어) is the official language of South Korea and North Korea and is also spoken in diaspora communities worldwide. It has its unique alphabet called Hangul.

Hangul is a phonetic alphabet designed in the 15th century, making it relatively easy to learn and pronounce. The language has a system of honorifics based on social hierarchy.

Korean grammar follows a subject-object-verb structure, and there are no articles or gendered nouns, though honorifics play a significant role in communication.

Learning Korean is useful for engaging with Korean pop culture (K-pop, K-dramas), technology, and business, as South Korea is a global powerhouse in these sectors.

In conclusion, Korean offers a window into a rich cultural and linguistic heritage, with growing global importance.
